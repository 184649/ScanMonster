# Phase 2C Contract and Rights Checklist

Status: Discussion checklist / not legal advice / 2026-07-18

## Important notice

This document is not legal advice and does not state which terms are legally sufficient or enforceable. It is a checklist for the contracting parties and appropriately qualified legal, tax, employment, privacy, or IP advisers. Applicable law, jurisdiction, working relationship, and intended use may require additional terms.

Record the agreed answer, responsible reviewer, contract clause, evidence, and unresolved questions for every applicable item.

## 1. Parties, scope, and work process

- Correct legal names, addresses, contacts, and contracting capacity of the parties.
- Independent-contractor/employment characterization to be professionally confirmed where relevant.
- Exact scope for Stage 0 through Stage 6 and what is excluded.
- Number of motifs, concepts, views, expressions, color directions, and source files.
- Whether motif replacement is in scope and its additional fee.
- Included revision rounds; definition of one revision; consolidation of feedback.
- Additional-revision rate, change-of-direction rate, and approval-response periods.
- Schedule, milestones, dependencies, delivery method, and treatment of delays.
- Fees, taxes, currency, invoices, deposits, milestone payments, and final payment.
- Cancellation, suspension, cure periods, and rescheduling.
- Rights and permissible use of work produced before cancellation or partial payment.
- Acceptance procedure and consequences of rejection or non-response.

## 2. Copyright ownership or license

- Whether copyright is assigned, licensed, or otherwise retained, as confirmed by qualified review.
- If licensed: exclusive or non-exclusive status.
- License or assignment effective date and any payment condition.
- Duration and renewal/termination consequences.
- Geographic territory.
- Permitted languages and media, including future media where appropriate.
- Right to use in the game/application.
- Web and landing-page use.
- Advertising and paid promotion.
- Social-media use.
- Video, streaming, broadcast, and trailer use.
- Books, printed matter, dex/reference publications, and editorial use.
- Merchandise generally.
- Plush-toy production.
- Figure, model, and collectible production.
- Events, exhibitions, retail displays, and live experiences.
- Sequels, updates, expansions, ports, and successor services.
- Derivative works, spin-offs, adaptations, and new character forms.
- Character names, working names, logos, and title treatments.
- Cooperation, signatures, and evidence needed for trademark applications or enforcement.
- Credit form, placement, exceptions, and whether credit is required.
- Sublicensing, manufacturing partners, publishers, platforms, and distributors.

## 3. Modification and production continuation

- Permission to modify, simplify, crop, recolor, localize, or combine the work.
- New poses, expressions, costumes, forms, and marketing adaptations.
- 3D modeling and figure engineering.
- Animation, rigging, deformation, and motion use.
- Plush simplification and manufacturing changes.
- Handoff to other artists, studios, manufacturers, or future vendors.
- Whether another artist may create new characters under the art bible.
- Designer consultation or approval rights for material changes, if any.
- Treatment of moral rights or comparable rights, subject to applicable-law review.

## 4. Source files and third-party material

- Required editable source formats, version compatibility, layer organization, and naming.
- Delivery of linked files, brushes, actions, color profiles, swatches, and export settings where permitted.
- Delivery of integrated PNG/vector/high-resolution outputs.
- Fonts used, font licenses, and whether editable text is converted or retained.
- Stock images, textures, brushes, models, plug-ins, references, templates, or other third-party materials.
- License evidence and redistribution limits for every third-party component.
- A representation or disclosure process for original authorship and authorized collaborators.
- Storage, backup, return, and deletion obligations for source and confidential materials.

## 5. Generative AI and future AI-assisted use

- Whether generative AI will be used at any stage, including ideation, inpainting, reference, upscaling, cleanup, or variation.
- If used: system/model and version, service terms, account type, dates, inputs, outputs, settings, and human-authored modifications as reasonably recordable.
- Whether client confidential material, existing art, third-party material, or personal data is uploaded.
- Source and rights basis for every input or reference supplied to an AI system.
- Review of relevant model/service terms and output-use conditions by the parties or advisers.
- Similarity, provenance, and third-party-rights review for AI-assisted outputs.
- Whether the commissioned deliverables may later be used as references or inputs for controlled AI-assisted production.
- Whether the deliverables, drafts, prompts, or source files may be used for model training, fine-tuning, embeddings, evaluation, or dataset creation.
- Separate permission boundaries for the client, designer, subcontractors, platforms, and the public.
- Opt-out, deletion, recordkeeping, security, and vendor-retention requirements.
- Whether AI-use disclosure is required at delivery and what evidence accompanies it.

No future AI training or reference permission should be inferred from ordinary art-delivery language. Record an explicit answer.

## 6. Confidentiality and publicity

- Confidential information definition, exclusions, permitted recipients, and security duties.
- Project codename, unpublished motifs, designs, scans, product data, and roadmap treatment.
- Embargo and public reveal date.
- Designer portfolio publication rights, timing, format, and approval process.
- Case studies, social posts, awards, livestreams, and process recordings.
- Return/deletion duties and archival exceptions.
- Duration and survival of confidentiality obligations.

## 7. Representations, similarity, and claims process

- Designer disclosure of collaborators, employers, licensors, and other obligations.
- Disclosure and approval of subcontractors.
- Process for checking whether the work resembles a third party's protected material.
- Scope within which similar concepts may be offered to other clients.
- Handling of pre-existing designer style, tools, templates, and portfolio elements.
- Notice, investigation, cooperation, defense, correction, replacement, suspension, and cost allocation if infringement is alleged or established.
- Warranties, indemnities, liability limits, insurance, and remedies, all subject to qualified legal review.
- Governing law, jurisdiction or dispute process, notices, assignment, and contract precedence.

## 8. Completion record

Before production use, attach or identify:

- the signed agreement and amendments;
- final statement of work and stage schedule;
- approval record for each stage;
- source-file inventory and checksum/version record;
- third-party-material and font disclosures;
- generative-AI disclosure;
- final rights/territory/media summary confirmed against the contract;
- confidentiality and portfolio-release dates;
- unresolved issues and the person responsible for professional review.

This checklist does not approve any right, use, or legal position. Production use begins only after the actual agreement and required professional review are complete.
