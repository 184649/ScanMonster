# Phase 2C Human Flagship Character Designer Brief

Status: External commission brief / design only / 2026-07-18

## 1. Project overview

**Project name:** WORLDAWN

WORLDAWN is a smartphone discovery experience. A user scans a barcode or QR code, encounters a creature or character, receives an official per-character discovery number, and creates a discovery certificate. The encounter is recorded in a dex, discovery history, and calendar; rediscovery and titles give later encounters meaning.

The central vocabulary is **discovery, encounter, record, rediscovery, discovery certificate, title, and dex**. WORLDAWN is not centered on combat, capture, training, ability strength, or unlocking fighters. The character must make the user feel: “I want to discover it,” “I am glad I met it,” “I want to keep it in my dex,” “I want to remember its name,” and “I want to meet it again.”

The product's scan, official-number, certificate, history, and dex foundations already exist. This commission designs a human-authored character baseline without changing those systems or assigning production character IDs.

## 2. Commercial goal

WORLDAWN aims to become an independent, long-lived original IP, not merely a polished animal encyclopedia. Its characters should remain desirable when seen beside successful character-collection IPs, while never copying a specific existing character, face, eyes, body proportion, palette, marking, pose, style, or silhouette.

The goal is comparable **quality of attachment and recognition**, not visual imitation. The first three characters, with an option to commission up to five reference characters after validation, must establish the baseline for the entire IP. Do not mass-produce the recommended future portfolio of 24 before the baseline characters and cross-character rules pass review.

## 3. Required character quality

Every approved reference character must:

- be recognizable from its black silhouette;
- leave a feature that can be described after a three-second view;
- suggest personality through posture and construction, before explanatory text;
- have a one-sentence reason someone may choose and like that individual;
- remain legible at 64 px and 128 px;
- be simple enough for a child or fan to draw in a reduced form;
- translate into a figure and a plush toy;
- use a species-specific face that cannot be exchanged with another animal;
- belong to the same IP across very different body structures without reusing one face, eye, or body template;
- remain identifiable without equipment, clothing, background, effects, texture, or a detailed rendering finish.

Each character should have one dominant memory point in its silhouette, one in its face construction, and one in its large color-plane arrangement. Supporting decisions must reinforce those points rather than compete with them.

## 4. Directions to avoid

Do not propose:

- a real animal with only light stylization;
- a generic “cute animal”;
- the same mascot face pasted onto different species;
- a mascot distinguished only by very large eyes or baby proportions;
- realistic animal illustration;
- fine fur, scales, skin, or rendering texture as the main attraction;
- clothing, armor, weapons, carried tools, or background effects as the source of identity;
- a face, body, palette, marking, pose, style, or silhouette that strongly recalls a specific famous character;
- differentiation made only by adding decoration;
- pose variants of one individual presented as independent concepts;
- personality added afterward as text to a neutral silhouette;
- one standard eye shape or smile applied to every character.

Reference to established IP may be used only to discuss abstract quality or similarity risk. Images of famous characters must not be used as imitation targets.

## 5. Rarity policy

The order is `normal`, `rare`, `legendary`, and `secret`. Rarity is not a quality ladder.

- **normal:** clear, approachable, one strong individual quality, and rewarding to discover repeatedly;
- **rare:** an unusual real organism, deep-sea or luminous organism, or distinctive ecology that gives a different surprise;
- **legendary:** mythology, folklore, fantasy organisms, UMA, or another world-significant presence;
- **secret:** a concealed exceptional category whose existence is not advertised.

A normal character must never be dull, generic, or merely a literal animal. The initial test concerns character language, not rarity escalation, and does not authorize final rarity assignments.

## 6. First commission scope

The default test uses three deliberately different structures:

| Category | Default motif | Structural purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Small and approachable ground animal | Hamster | Compact mammal, affection, face appeal, plush structure |
| Large ground animal with presence | Elephant | Mass, trunk, ears, four supports, gentle authority |
| Non-mammal / limbless animal | Snake | Curves, balance, head transition, personality without limbs |

The designer may propose replacement motifs if these three are too weak to establish the IP. A replacement must preserve the three tests: a small friendly ground animal, a large ground animal with presence, and a non-mammal or otherwise clearly different body structure. The three structures must not overlap; sheep is excluded. As a rule, choose from existing `initial` / `ground` / `normal` candidates, explain the character and IP reason for the change, do not change merely because another animal is easier to draw, and assess similarity risk to existing famous IP.

A motif change is a proposal only. It is not confirmed without user approval. No official character name or production `characterId` is assigned during this commission package.

## 7. Expected designer role

The commission is character design and IP-system design, not simply polished animal illustration. The designer is expected to:

- articulate a one-sentence core and reason to like each character;
- convert personality and behavior into body construction and posture;
- transform species-specific anatomy into a memorable design point;
- design silhouettes before faces, color, or finish;
- define a face language that is shared in principle but not reused as one face;
- define which shape rules are common to the three and which must vary;
- anticipate figures, plush toys, small icons, model sheets, and future animation;
- produce rules robust enough to guide 24 or more future characters across mammals and non-mammals;
- identify and document prohibited transformations and likely similarity risks.

## 8. Phase boundary and decision ownership

The first paid contract unit should cover Stage 0 creative direction and Stage 1 black silhouettes. Each Stage 1 option must be an independent character proposal, not a pose variation. The user selects up to two silhouettes per motif. If the user cannot select one, the parties follow the agreed revision or stop terms; the designer does not proceed automatically to a face or final illustration.

Only the user can decide whether a character feels likable, worth completing, or capable of standing beside popular characters. Technical review and internal rubrics support that decision but do not replace it. Stage 2 and later work requires explicit approval under the staged acceptance document.

## 9. Preserved WORLDAWN architecture

Biological `SpeciesMotif` material and IP `CharacterIdentity` remain separate. One species need not equal one character; a character may use a small number of purposeful motifs. Existing IDs, discoveries, certificates, official discovery numbers, rarity, and release status are outside this commission and must not be renamed, repurposed, or treated as design variables.

The commission establishes a human-authored reference baseline only. Production data registration, migration, runtime integration, and release are separate future decisions.
