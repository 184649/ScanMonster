# Phase 2C Flagship Character Briefs

Status: External design briefs / motifs and wording are provisional / 2026-07-18

## Shared assignment

The three flagships test whether one WORLDAWN design language can support radically different structures without applying one face, eye, smile, body ratio, or pose formula to all of them. They must feel rewarding to discover and remember; combat roles and powers are not the design premise.

For every category, the designer must provide a one-sentence character core, a one-sentence reason to like the individual, one primary silhouette memory point, one face-construction memory point, and one large color-plane memory point. The hypotheses below are starting questions, not approved names, final identities, or production IDs.

Shared principles to preserve:

- design from character core and black silhouette before face, color, or finish;
- express personality through weight, posture, proportion, and species-specific structure;
- remain legible at 64 px and 128 px;
- remain drawable in a simplified fan or child drawing;
- translate into approximately 8–12 major three-dimensional blocks at final acceptance;
- avoid equipment, clothing, background, texture, and effects as identity;
- avoid concrete resemblance to an existing famous character;
- make the trio one IP through consistent abstraction and graphic discipline, not a shared face template.

## 1. Small and approachable

### Category purpose and default motif

**Default motif:** hamster.

This category tests small-scale approachability, face appeal, affection, and plush translation without relying on giant round eyes, infant proportions, or a generic pet photograph. It should prove that a normal, familiar animal can have an individual character identity.

### Alternative conditions

A replacement may be proposed only if it is a small, approachable ground organism that offers a stronger character premise and a structure distinct from the large and non-mammal categories. Prefer an existing `initial` / `ground` / `normal` candidate. Sheep is excluded. The proposal must explain the IP benefit, character core, structural test, and similarity risk; “easier to draw” is insufficient. User approval is required before replacement.

### IP question

Can a compact familiar mammal create affection through construction, restraint, and voluntary behavior—not through generic cuteness, oversized eyes, or baby coding—and still work as a plush toy?

### Character-core requirement

The core must join a small physical action to an emotionally legible choice. Phase 2A's provisional question was a cautious individual gathering courage to share something it values. The designer may replace that premise, but the final core must be visible in posture and must not require a prop.

### Reason to like

The reason must describe why someone would choose this individual rather than “because it is cute.” A viable starting hypothesis is affection for a tiny but voluntary act of courage or generosity.

### Memory points and construction

- **Silhouette:** one contrast between compact body mass and an intentional forward or withheld gesture; cheeks alone are not the hook.
- **Face:** a hamster-specific brow, short muzzle, cheek, and head transition that cannot be pasted onto another small mammal; eye size is secondary.
- **Color plane:** one simple face/body or face/forelimb relationship using three or four reproducible regions; fine markings are not the identity.
- **Toy blocks:** initially test head, main body/hind mass, forelimb gesture, and foot/support blocks; reorganize as needed while keeping the final form within roughly 8–12 major blocks.
- **Small-screen survivors:** head-to-body ratio, ear placement, muzzle/cheek transition, primary posture, and the single silhouette hook.

### Difference from the other two

It supplies the smallest scale, softest mass, and closest emotional distance. It must not borrow the elephant's heavy column support or the snake's continuous coil logic.

### Failures to avoid

- inflated cheek pouches as the only idea;
- standing, sitting, or eating as pose-only variations;
- a body distinguished only by roundness;
- a generic mascot face;
- human-like hands or acting;
- clothes, pouches, food, or carried props;
- giant eyes or infant proportions as the sole source of affection.

## 2. Large and present

### Category purpose and default motif

**Default motif:** elephant.

This category tests large-character presence, a powerful silhouette, and meaningful use of trunk, ears, head/shoulder mass, body weight, and four supports. It must express a welcome kind of authority without escaping into muscle, armor, realistic animal rendering, or fear.

### Alternative conditions

A replacement may be proposed only if it is a large ground organism with clear mass and support architecture that remains structurally distinct from the small and non-mammal categories. Prefer an existing `initial` / `ground` / `normal` candidate; sheep is excluded. The designer must explain why the replacement tests WORLDAWN better and how it avoids famous-IP similarity. User approval is mandatory.

### IP question

Can mass, trunk/appendage behavior, ear/head construction, and deliberate weight shift create a gentle, memorable character without making a realistic calf, warrior, armored creature, or frightening giant?

### Character-core requirement

The core must turn size into a particular relationship with discovery or others. Phase 2A's provisional question was a patient guide who listens to the path before taking one certain step. Another core is welcome if it is more distinctive and visible without tools or decoration.

### Reason to like

The reason must be more specific than strength or cuteness. A viable starting hypothesis is trust in a powerful individual whose care and certainty are expressed through deliberate action.

### Memory points and construction

- **Silhouette:** one memorable relationship among trunk, head/shoulder mass, body weight, and supports; trunk length or ear size alone is insufficient.
- **Face:** an elephant-specific forehead, trunk root, cheek, ear-base, and jaw construction rather than a human face placed between ears.
- **Color plane:** a small number of large, merchandise-safe regions that follow the trunk/head/body construction; no ceremonial markings or surface ornament.
- **Toy blocks:** test head/shoulder, body, trunk, ear planes, and four support groups, then consolidate into roughly 8–12 stable blocks.
- **Small-screen survivors:** head/shoulder mass, trunk direction and root, ear/head relationship, support rhythm, and the primary weight shift.

### Difference from the other two

It carries the greatest mass and ground contact, with multiple vertical supports and a directional appendage. It must not become a scaled-up rounded hamster or a curve-only snake.

### Failures to avoid

- changing only trunk length;
- changing only ear size;
- front/side orientation variants presented as concepts;
- a generic elephant pictogram;
- using only calf proportions to create warmth;
- muscles, armor, aggression, or frightening scale as identity;
- ritual decoration, cloth, saddle, or crown as the hook.

## 3. Non-mammal and limbless

### Category purpose and default motif

**Default motif:** snake.

This category tests character personality without hands or legs, identity through curves, balance, negative space, head/neck transition, and limbless toy construction. It must not depend only on coil pattern or facial expression.

### Alternative conditions

A replacement may be proposed if it is non-mammalian or has a clearly different body structure from both ground mammals. Prefer an existing `initial` / `ground` / `normal` candidate. The three structures must remain non-overlapping; sheep is excluded. The designer must explain the stronger IP test, toy solution, and similarity risks. User approval is required before changing the motif.

### IP question

Can a limbless or radically non-mammalian body express reassuring, individual personality through center of mass, curve hierarchy, negative space, and head construction—without adopting mammal facial shorthand?

### Character-core requirement

The core must give stillness, movement, or attention a specific emotional meaning. Phase 2A's provisional question was a quiet listener who notices ground-borne signals and makes space feel safe. The designer may replace it, but not with generic mystery, poison, villainy, or magic.

### Reason to like

The reason must make this individual welcome rather than merely novel or threatening. A viable starting hypothesis is feeling understood by an attentive presence whose stillness reads as care.

### Memory points and construction

- **Silhouette:** one structural relationship among ground mass, directional rise, negative space, and head wedge; a different coil alone is not enough.
- **Face:** a species-specific wedge, jaw, snout, and neck transition; do not use mammal cheeks, eyelashes, or oversized eyes.
- **Color plane:** three or four broad regions following body direction and head transition; no jewels, spots, scales, or ornamental pattern as the hook.
- **Toy blocks:** organize the continuous body into a stable base, counter-mass, directional section, neck transition, and head, then prove a self-supporting roughly 8–12-block simplification.
- **Small-screen survivors:** head-to-neck transition, main ground mass, one directional rise, one intentional negative space, and tail termination.

### Difference from the other two

It removes limbs and separate torso supports, forcing the shared IP language to survive in a continuous body. It must not inherit the same eyes and smile used for the mammals.

### Failures to avoid

- changing only the coil arrangement;
- changing only neck length;
- a generic snake symbol;
- differentiation using only a ring, spiral, or S curve;
- fangs, poison, cobra threat, or villain coding;
- dragon conversion;
- jewelry, gems, magic effects, or surface pattern as identity.

The default motifs and all starting hypotheses remain design inputs. They do not establish official names, production IDs, rarity, release status, or art. Any motif replacement and every stage transition require the approvals defined in `PHASE2C_DELIVERABLES_AND_ACCEPTANCE.md`.
