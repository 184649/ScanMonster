# Phase 2C Designer Selection Rubric

Status: Internal reference only / does not automate hiring / 2026-07-18

## 1. Use of this rubric

This 100-point rubric supports consistent portfolio review, interviews, and—when needed—a small paid test. It does not automatically select or reject a designer. Scores must be accompanied by evidence, uncertainty, availability, communication fit, rights/contract feasibility, and the user's judgment. Do not ask candidates to complete the full commission as an unpaid competition.

If portfolios do not provide enough process evidence, request clarification or propose a narrowly scoped paid Stage 0/Stage 1 test. A generated image alone, without an explainable design process and rights basis, is not sufficient evidence.

## 2. Scoring table

Use 0–5 for each criterion, then multiply by the listed factor. Record links or work samples rather than scoring from memory.

| Category | Weight | 0 | 3 | 5 | Evidence to record |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Character-construction fundamentals | 25 | Anatomy/proportion inconsistent; finish hides weak construction | Competent forms with some memorable decisions | Strong, reproducible construction with clear individual identity before finish | Roughs, turnarounds, construction breakdowns; score ×5 |
| Different body structures | 15 | One body/face template | Some range, mainly adjacent mammals | Convincing mammals, non-mammals, limbless/atypical structures without style loss | Cross-species lineup; score ×3 |
| Silhouette design | 15 | Pose/outline variants only | Several readable shapes, uneven memory points | Independent concepts with clear mass, negative space, and small-size readability | Black-shape exploration and thumbnails; score ×3 |
| Personality translated into shape | 15 | Personality exists only in text/expression/props | Some posture/proportion linkage | Core, behavior, and reason to like are visible in body logic | Brief-to-shape rationale; score ×3 |
| Model sheets and repeatability | 10 | Finished front image only | Basic turnaround with minor inconsistencies | Consistent views, expressions, rules, prohibited deformations, production handoff | Front/side/rear/3/4 sheets; score ×2 |
| Toy and merchandise potential | 10 | Depends on fine detail or impossible volume | Can simplify with compromises | Clear major blocks, plush/figure awareness, small icon survival | Product examples or block studies; score ×2 |
| Originality and existing-IP avoidance | 10 | Strongly derivative or no risk awareness | Generally original with limited analysis | Independent visual reasoning and an explicit similarity-avoidance process | Original projects, references policy, risk notes; score ×2 |

Maximum: **100 points**.

## 3. Portfolio review checklist

Confirm whether the portfolio demonstrates:

- roughs and exploration, not only finished illustrations;
- different faces rather than one face reused across species;
- successful non-mammals and structurally unusual bodies;
- consistent front, side, rear, and three-quarter views;
- expression variants that preserve construction;
- the ability to explain why a design decision exists;
- a process that begins with silhouette or construction;
- awareness of game, animation, figure, plush, and other merchandise uses;
- substantial original work, not only fan art for existing IP;
- a traceable creative process rather than unexplained generated-AI images;
- small-size examples or icon literacy;
- collaboration history, feedback handling, and source-file discipline.

Absence of one item is a question, not an automatic failure. Record what was actually observed and what must be verified in interview or test.

## 4. Interview and proposal questions

1. How would you make three radically different bodies feel like one IP without one shared face?
2. Show a case where personality changed proportion, center of mass, or negative space.
3. How do you prevent eight silhouette concepts from becoming pose variants?
4. How do you review similarity to existing characters?
5. What must be defined before another artist can reproduce your character?
6. How do you simplify a design for 64 px, plush, and figure production?
7. Which tools, third-party materials, and generative-AI systems, if any, enter your process?
8. What review cadence and source-file structure do you prefer?

## 5. Paid-test recommendation

When portfolios are promising but not decisive, use a small paid test covering Stage 0 creative direction and a limited, mutually agreed portion of Stage 1. State the fee, schedule, option count, revision count, permitted uses, ownership/license status, confidentiality, and stop condition before work. Do not request finished color art as the first test and do not imply that unpaid speculative work is required for consideration.

The final selection remains a human decision. The numerical total may expose strengths and gaps, but it cannot determine affection, trust, collaboration quality, originality as a legal conclusion, or contract acceptability.
